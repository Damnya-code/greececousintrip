# Editorial voice for day pages

Write for three cousins following a nearly finished, professionally planned itinerary.

- Aim for 75% polished travel guide and 25% restrained, dry humour.
- Open with the places, food, transport or experiences the day actually offers.
- Keep main introductions to approximately 50–75 words.
- Use no more than one light joke per section. Keep it warm, specific and understated.
- Prefer concrete names, timings and logistics over abstract emotional language.
- Let photography carry the drama; copy should sound confident and human.
- Avoid generic travel language such as “an unfolding story,” “one perfect day,” “where magic happens,” “the trip announces itself,” and routine claims that places are iconic, unforgettable or breathtaking.
- Avoid negative-opening constructions such as “This is not...” or “There is no race...”

Reference line:

> The difficult part is getting three cousins into the same city at roughly the same time. Once the bags are down, the plan is simple—walk toward the old city, catch the sunset, order too many shared plates in Psirri and call the trip officially started.
